# High-Pressure Feed Pump — Operation & Maintenance Manual (Excerpt)

- Document ID: MAN-HPP-001
- Asset class: High-pressure RO feed pump (multistage centrifugal)
- Applies to: AST-HPP-01, AST-HPP-02 (KSB Multitec-HP class)
- Status: Synthetic reference excerpt for advisory use only.

## 1. Rated operating envelope

| Parameter | Rated | Alarm |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Discharge pressure | 60–70 bar | > 75 bar |
| Flow (BEP) | 500 m³/h | < 330 m³/h (low-flow recirculation) |
| Drive-end bearing temperature | ≤ 85 °C | > 90 °C |
| Winding temperature | ≤ 150 °C | > 155 °C |
| Vibration (RMS velocity, ISO 10816) | ≤ 4.5 mm/s | > 4.5 mm/s |
| Mechanical seal leakage | < 1 mL/min | > 2 mL/min |

Operating continuously below the minimum-flow point causes hydraulic
recirculation, temperature rise and accelerated bearing/seal wear. Sustained
operation above the pressure alarm risks seal failure.

## 2. Degradation signatures

A progressive rise in vibration together with an efficiency drift below the
commissioning baseline typically indicates **drive-end bearing wear** or
**impeller/wear-ring erosion**. A step increase in seal leakage indicates
**mechanical-seal cartridge** wear. These signatures reduce hydraulic
efficiency and raise specific energy consumption before any hard failure.

## 3. Recommended maintenance

- Replace the drive-end bearing set and mechanical seal cartridge at the planned
  overhaul interval (nominally 365 operating days) or earlier if vibration or
  seal-leakage alarms persist.
- Confirm the standby pump (AST-HPP-02) is aligned and available before taking a
  duty pump offline.

> Advisory only. This excerpt does not authorize any control action. Isolation
> and maintenance require operator approval and the pump isolation procedure.
