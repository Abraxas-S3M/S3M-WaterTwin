# Procedure: RO Membrane Clean-In-Place (CIP)

- Document ID: PROC-CIP-MEMB-001
- Applies to: AST-MEMB-01 (RO membrane array, Train 1)
- Type: Cleaning procedure
- Status: Synthetic reference procedure for advisory use only.

## When to clean

Field practice triggers a CIP when the normalized indices cross screening
thresholds:

- Normalized differential pressure rise ≥ 15% vs the clean baseline, or
- Normalized salt-passage rise ≥ 10% vs the clean baseline, or
- Normalized permeate-flow decline ≥ 10% vs the clean baseline.

Scaling (CaCO₃, CaSO₄, BaSO₄, SrSO₄, silica) concentrates in the tail elements
of the last stage; organic/colloidal/biofouling loads hit the lead elements
first. Match the cleaning chemistry to the dominant fouling mechanism.

## Steps (summary)

1. Obtain operator approval; isolate the train per the applicable isolation
   procedure.
2. Low-pH clean (citric/HCl) to remove carbonate/metal scale; circulate at the
   vendor-specified flow and temperature.
3. High-pH clean (NaOH + surfactant) to remove organic/biological fouling.
4. Flush to drain, then soak/recirculate as needed for stubborn fouling.
5. Rinse and return to service; record normalized dP, salt passage and permeate
   flow to confirm recovery of performance.

## Verification

A successful CIP restores the normalized indices toward baseline. If salt
passage does not recover, evaluate membrane integrity / element replacement.

> Advisory only. Cleaning chemicals and pressures are hazardous; execution is a
> human-approved field activity. The platform recommends and audits only.
