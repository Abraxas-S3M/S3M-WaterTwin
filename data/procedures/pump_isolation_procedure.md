# Procedure: High-Pressure Pump Isolation (Lockout/Tagout)

- Document ID: PROC-ISO-HPP-001
- Applies to: AST-HPP-01, AST-HPP-02
- Type: Isolation / lockout-tagout procedure
- Status: Synthetic reference procedure for advisory use only.

## Preconditions

1. Confirm the standby high-pressure pump (AST-HPP-02) is aligned, primed and
   available to carry duty, or that the train is scheduled down.
2. Verify product-water demand can be met from storage or the parallel train
   during the isolation window.

## Steps

1. Obtain operator approval and open a work order referencing this procedure.
2. Ramp the duty pump to minimum safe flow, then stop it at the local control
   station. Do **not** issue a remote stop from the advisory platform — it has no
   control-write path.
3. Close the suction isolation valve, then the discharge isolation valve.
4. Apply lockout/tagout to the motor disconnect and both isolation valves.
5. Bleed trapped high pressure through the drain to atmospheric, confirming
   0 bar on the local gauge before any mechanical work.
6. Verify zero energy state and record it on the work order.

## Restoration

Reverse the isolation steps, confirm seal flush and bearing lubrication, and
return the pump to standby before restoring it to duty.

> Advisory only. Every step requires a qualified operator; the platform records
> the recommendation and approval but never actuates equipment.
